Project Summary

Context:
Haro Hotel, a futuristic Japanese-style resort, experienced a surge in booking cancellations from 15% in 2017 to 37% in 2018. This increase put approximately ‚Ç¨4.02M in annual revenue at risk. The trend was structurally driven by the digitalization of room reservations, increasing booking flexibility but lowering customer commitment.

Goals:
Reduce the cancellation rate from 37% to 20% within six months by identifying key cancellation drivers, predicting high-risk bookings, and designing targeted revenue recovery strategies.

Process:
The project followed a structured analytics workflow: business understanding ‚Üí data preparation ‚Üí cleaning ‚Üí exploratory data analysis (EDA). Diagnostic analysis was conducted using Python‚Äôs statsmodels to identify key drivers. A tree-based classification model (Gradient Boosting with GridSearch CV) was developed to predict high-risk bookings.

Output:
The model detects 77% of cancellations, enabling proactive interventions such as milestone-based confirmations and partial deposits for high-risk bookings. Retaining 60% of predicted cancellations would reduce the overall rate to 20% and recover over 35% of at-risk revenue.

Scope of Work / Achievements

  • Developed a Gradient Boosting classification model detecting 77% of high-risk cancellations.
  • Identified five key cancellation drivers explaining 83% of total risk.
  • Analyzed 2018 performance showing a 21.9% YoY increase, risking ‚Ç¨4.02M annual revenue.
  • Designed a retention strategy projected to recover 35% of at-risk revenue by reducing cancellation rate to 20%.
