Hotel Cancellation Prediction

A machine learning solution transforming cancellation risk into measurable revenue recovery.

Reduce cancellations: 37% → 20%

77% recall using Gradient Boosting
View Project Repo

Project Summary

Context: Haro Hotel experienced a surge in booking cancellations from 15% in 2017 to 37% in 2018. This increase put approximately €4.02M in annual revenue at risk. The trend is structurally driven by the digitalization of room reservations, which offers high flexibility but results in low customer commitment.

Goal: The primary objective was to reduce the hotel booking cancellation rate from 37% to 20% within a six-month period. This involved identifying key factors driving cancellations, predicting future cancellation risks for upcoming reservations, and strategizing targeted interventions to recover at-risk revenue.

Process: The analytical approach followed a structured workflow: business understanding, data preparation, cleaning, and exploratory data analysis (EDA). Diagnostic analysis was performed using Python’s statsmodel to identify drivers, followed by developing a tree-based classification machine learning model (Gradient Boosting with GridSearch CV) to predict high-risk bookings.

Output: The model successfully detects 77% of cancellations, enabling proactive interventions such as milestone-based confirmations and partial deposits for high-risk bookings. Retaining 60% of these predicted cancellations could reduce the overall rate to 20% and recover over 35% of at-risk revenue. These data-led strategies turn cancellation management into a controllable strategic lever.

Scope of Work

  • Developed a tree-based Gradient Boosting classification model to detect 77% of high-risk cancellations for upcoming hotel reservations.
  • Identified five key cancellation drivers—including online bookings and long lead times—explaining 83% of total cancellation risk.
  • Analyzed 2018 performance to reveal a 21.9% year-over-year cancellation increase, putting €4.02M in annual revenue at risk.
  • Formulated a retention strategy to recover 35% of at-risk revenue by reducing the final cancellation rate to 20%.
